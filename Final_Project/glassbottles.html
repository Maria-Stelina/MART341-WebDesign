Glass Bottle Flower Vases

Don't throw away your wine bottles. Repurpose them instead! They can upgrade your home in several different ways. In this case here, I used a leftover wine bottle from Easter, and a bottle that ran out of balsamic vinegar as flower vases. The rims at the top were small but it left just enough room to fit in 5 dollar roses in each bottle. The total arrangment was just 10 dollars and the results are very elegant and inexpensive! Instead of buying a $50 bouquet the next time you go to a flower shop, consider making your own arrangment! As you can see it doesn't take much to make a beautiful flower arrangment.

Here are some tips for good flower arrangments:


  • Pluck out some of the leaves from the end and middle of the stems so that the flowers look more neat
  • Trim the ends at a 45 degree angle. It helps the flowers absorb the water better
  • Use a rubber band to bunch the flowers together if they spread apart too much
  • Add ice in the water. It helps keep the freshness longer!
